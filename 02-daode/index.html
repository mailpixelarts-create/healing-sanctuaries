Questions

How does sound actually heal?

Through entrainment. Your brainwaves synchronise to external frequencies. A 432 Hz bowl shifts you from beta to alpha in 8 minutes. This is measurable with EEG. The sound creates the conditions for your body's parasympathetic nervous system to engage, which triggers your innate healing response.

Is it just sitting and listening to bowls?

No. Eva uses a combination of Tibetan singing bowls (placed on the body), tuning forks (applied to acupressure points), and binaural beats (delivered through headphones). Each tool targets different frequencies and different healing outcomes. It is a multi-layered sonic architecture.

What if I fall asleep?

That is one of the best outcomes. Sleep means your conscious mind has stepped aside, allowing the frequencies to work directly with your subconscious and nervous system. Many of the deepest healings happen during sleep. You will still receive the full benefit.

Can sound therapy help with tinnitus?

Yes. Counterintuitively, specific frequencies can reduce tinnitus perception. The bowls do not add noise — they create a coherent frequency landscape that your brain can entrain to, reducing the chaotic signal that causes tinnitus. Eva has a specific protocol for tinnitus sufferers.

How is this different from a sound bath?

A sound bath is usually a group experience with general frequencies. Eva's sessions are one-to-one with precision frequency mapping. She diagnoses which chakras are blocked and selects bowls and forks accordingly. This is sonic surgery, not a bath.

What if I'm sceptical?

Perfect. Scepticism means you are paying attention. You do not need to believe in sound therapy for it to work. The frequencies affect your nervous system regardless of your beliefs. Many of Eva's most transformed clients started as sceptics. Come with your doubts. Leave with data.
