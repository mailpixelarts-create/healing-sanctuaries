Alveolar CO₂ Holding the breath and extending the exhale builds carbon dioxide in the alveoli. This CO₂ is the signal that triggers vasodilation — the widening of blood vessels. More CO₂ means more blood flow to the brain.
Prefrontal Cortex The brain's executive centre — decision-making, impulse control, emotional regulation. When it receives 40% more oxygen, anxiety cannot sustain itself. You think clearly. You choose instead of react.

FAQs

I feel dizzy and my hands tingle. Is this normal?
Yes. This is hypocapnia — low CO₂ in your blood. The tingling is alkalosis. It is not dangerous. It is just your body's pH shifting. If the dizziness becomes overwhelming, slow down and breathe into a paper bag or your hands. This will increase CO₂ and normalise the sensation. It is a sign the protocol is working.
Can I do breathwork alone?
We strongly advise against it for Holotropic or hyperventilation techniques. Without a guide, you can enter a dissociative state or panic. The Wim Hof method is safer for solo practice, but even then, never do it in water (shower, bath, pool). Blackout risk is real. Always have a spotter. The 4-7-8 technique is safe for daily solo practice once learned.
Will breathwork give me a trip or hallucination?
No. It is not psychedelic. It can cause 'non-ordinary' states — where old memories surface, or you see colours, or you feel intense emotion. But you remain conscious and in control. You are just clearing the filter. The 'trip' is your own subconscious — not a drug. Think of it as opening a window in a stuffy room.
How does extended exhale reduce anxiety?
The 4-7-8 breath (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8) increases alveolar CO₂, which vasodilates the brain's microcapillaries, delivering 40% more oxygen to the prefrontal cortex. It also stimulates the vagus nerve, triggering the parasympathetic 'rest and digest' response. It is a chemical switch, not a psychological trick. The numbers don't lie.
Can I do breathwork if I have high blood pressure?
Yes, but with caution. Avoid extended breath holds (Kumbhaka). Stick to slow, rhythmic breathing without retention. Always consult your cardiologist. Breathwork lowers blood pressure in most — but retention can spike it temporarily. We modify the protocol for hypertension patients. Safety is non-negotiable.
Why do I cry so much during breathwork?
Breathing deeply moves the diaphragm, which massages the solar plexus — a major nerve plexus that stores suppressed emotion. When you unlock that muscle, you unlock the memory held in the tissue. Tears are the release valve. Do not hold them back. They are not sadness; they are liberation. The body remembers what the mind has forgotten.
