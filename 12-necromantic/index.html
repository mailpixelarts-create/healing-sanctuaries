Questions

Frequently Asked

“What is shamanic journeying?”
Shamanic journeying is a practice of entering trance states through rhythmic drumming to access non-ordinary reality. In these states, you encounter spirit allies, ancestors, and symbolic landscapes that offer healing, guidance, and forgotten wisdom.
“Am I asleep during the journey?”
No. You are between states — conscious enough to observe, relaxed enough to receive. The Theta state is not sleep; it is a doorway. Most people remember their journey vividly afterward.
“What if I see nothing?”
Sensations count. A feeling of warmth, a color, a sound, a sense of presence — these are all valid journey experiences. Not all visions come as images. Trust what arises.
“Is shamanic journeying safe?”
With a trained practitioner, yes. Carlos holds a sacred container throughout the journey, ensuring your spirit returns safely. The drum acts as an anchor, and the practice has been used for thousands of years across cultures.
“Can I journey alone?”
Yes, but guidance is safer to prevent dissociation. A trained practitioner holds the container so you can journey without losing yourself. Solo practice is recommended only after several guided experiences.
“What is soul retrieval?”
When trauma fragments the soul, parts of us become lost in other worlds. Soul retrieval is the practice of journeying to retrieve these lost soul fragments, restoring wholeness and vitality to the wounded spirit.
