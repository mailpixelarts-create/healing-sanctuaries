The Living Lineage of Rumi's Heart

Sheikh Rashid is an initiate of the Mevlevi Order, carrying the lineage of Rumi himself through an unbroken chain of spiritual transmission spanning over seven centuries. He received his training in the great tekkes of Konya, where Rumi is buried and where the whirling dervishes have prayed since the thirteenth century.

In the Sufi tradition, illness arises from forgetfulness of the Divine — from the illusion that we are separate from the Source of all being. Dhikr, the repetition of God's names and attributes through breath, movement, and song, is the medicine that restores the heart's connection to the Beloved.

Sheikh Rashid's healing sanctuary in Konya sits within walking distance of the Mevlana Museum, the tomb of Rumi. Here, he leads dhikr circles, sama ceremonies, and individual healing sessions that dissolve the illusions of separation that cause suffering. His teaching is simple: remember, and be healed.

The Sacred Art

Healing Modalities

01

Heart-centered Dhikr

Seated meditation repeating Divine names with deep feeling. The practitioner guides the client through the 99 Names of God, each name carrying a specific healing frequency. As the names are repeated with sincere devotion, the heart opens and the illusion of separation dissolves, revealing the light that was always present.

02

Whirling Ceremony

Sufi whirling as moving meditation and spiritual ascent. The sama, or whirling ceremony, is not performance — it is prayer in motion. As the body spins, the ego dissolves, the mind quiets, and the soul ascends through the spheres of consciousness toward union with the Divine. Each revolution strips away another layer of illusion.

03

Sama Healing Circle

Sound healing through devotional singing and sacred music. The Sama circle combines the Ney flute, frame drums, and chanted poetry of Rumi to create a sonic environment that bypasses the rational mind and speaks directly to the heart. In this space, tears flow freely and the heart remembers its true home.
