About the Healing Practice

Sheikh Rashid al-Qadiri is an initiate of the Mevlevi Order, carrying the sacred lineage of Jalal ad-Din Rumi himself. For over three decades, he has devoted his life to the practice and teaching of Dhikr — the remembrance of God through breath, movement, and devotional song.

In the Sufi tradition, all illness arises from forgetfulness of the Divine. When the heart remembers its Creator, healing flows naturally — not as a cure imposed from without, but as a remembering of the wholeness that was never truly lost.

Dhikr is the repetition of God's names and attributes — Ya Shafi (The Healer), Ya Rahman (The Merciful), Ya Wadud (The Loving) — through synchronized breathing, gentle movement, and sacred melody. Each breath becomes a prayer. Each heartbeat, a repetition of the Divine Name.
