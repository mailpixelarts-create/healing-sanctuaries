# 08 — Ayurveda: Raw Earth Academic
## Image Prompts

### Hero Image — Prakriti Compass
A top-down photograph of a brass compass lying on aged parchment paper. The compass needle points not to North, but to one of three labeled points: VATA (air/space), PITTA (fire/water), KAPHA (earth/water). Each point is a different color — Vata is blue-grey, Pitta is amber-red, Kapha is green-brown. The compass is ornate, antique, with Sanskrit characters etched into the bezel. The parchment beneath has faded botanical illustrations of herbs. The overall aesthetic is raw earth meets academic — like an 18th-century physician's desk. The lighting is warm, directional, casting long shadows. 16:9 aspect ratio.

### About Section — Dr. Vikram Portrait
A portrait of a man in his 60s, seated in a traditional Ayurvedic clinic. He wears a simple white kurta. His hands are on a patient's wrist — he is reading the pulse (Nadi Pariksha). His eyes are closed in concentration. The clinic is warm wood, with shelves of glass jars containing herbs and powders. A brass oil vessel sits on the desk. The light is natural, coming from a window to his left. He looks like a man who has spent decades listening to bodies. Medium close-up, warm tones. 3:2 aspect ratio.

### Mechanism Section — The Dosha System
An infographic showing the three Doshas as overlapping circles (Venn diagram style). Vata (air/space) is blue, Pitta (fire/water) is red-orange, Kapha (earth/water) is green. Each circle contains its associated qualities: Vata = dry, light, cold, mobile. Pitta = hot, sharp, oily, mobile. Kapha = heavy, slow, oily, stable. The overlapping areas show shared qualities. The background is warm parchment (#E8DDD0). The style is academic but accessible — like a well-designed textbook illustration. 16:9 aspect ratio.

### Services Section — Three Cards
Three vertical panels showing Ayurvedic practices:
1. **Tongue Scraping**: A close-up of a copper tongue scraper being drawn across a tongue. The copper catches warm light. The image is clinical but not unappealing — it looks like a morning ritual, not a medical procedure.
2. **Oil Pulling**: A person swishing coconut oil in their mouth, cheeks slightly puffed. The person is in a bathroom, morning light visible. The oil glistens. The ritual is mundane but sacred.
3. **Abhyanga (Self-Oil Massage)**: A person pouring warm sesame oil from a brass vessel onto their forearm. The oil is golden, viscous, catching the light. The pour is slow, deliberate. The body is oiled, glowing.
Raw earth palette — brown, gold, cream. 3:1 triptych.

### Process Section — Three Steps
Three images showing the daily Ayurvedic routine:
1. **Morning (Brahma Muhurta)**: A person rising before dawn, the sky outside the window a deep blue-grey. They reach for a copper water vessel on the bedside table. The room is still, quiet, dark. The day begins before the world does.
2. **Midday (Pachakala)**: A person eating a simple meal — rice, dal, vegetables — from a brass thali. The food is warm, steaming. The person eats with their hands, slowly, attentively. The meal is medicine.
3. **Evening (Sandhya)**: A person sitting cross-legged on the floor, eyes closed, a small flame (diya) burning in front of them. The room is dark except for the flame. The flame flickers. The day ends in stillness.
Warm earth tones throughout. 1:1 per image.

No people. The pharmacy holds the knowledge of generations. 16:9 aspect ratio.
