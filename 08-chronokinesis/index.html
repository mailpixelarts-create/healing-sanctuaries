Every imbalance manifests first in the microbiome, then in the immune response, then in symptoms. Ayurveda intervenes at the root — the terrain, not the weeds.

08 — Frequently Asked Questions

Common Inquiries

"Is this just an Indian diet?"
No. It is a constitutional framework. Ayurveda works with bio-individuality, not cultural identity. The same Vata-pacifying principles apply whether you live in Rishikesh or Rotterdam.
"Can I determine my Dosha online?"
It takes a pulse-reading. Online quizzes offer a starting point, but true Prakriti assessment requires Nadi Pariksha — the diagnostic art of reading pulse qualities that correlate with dosha composition.
"Can I have coffee?"
Only if you are Vata. Pitta and Kapha cannot. Coffee aggravates Pitta's inflammatory tendencies and creates dependency in Kapha's already sluggish metabolism. Vata can handle moderate amounts — in the morning, with warm milk.
"How long before I see results?"
Ayurveda works with your body's natural cycles. Most people notice shifts within 2–4 weeks. Deep constitutional change takes 3–6 months. This is not a quick fix — it is a permanent recalibration.
"Is Panchakarma safe for everyone?"
Panchakarma is prescribed only after thorough assessment. Contraindications include pregnancy, certain cardiac conditions, and acute illness. Dr. Sharma evaluates each candidate individually before recommending purification therapy.
"Can I follow Ayurveda alongside modern medicine?"
Yes, with transparency. Always inform both practitioners of all treatments. Ayurvedic herbs can interact with pharmaceuticals — particularly blood thinners, thyroid medications, and diabetes drugs. Coordination is essential.
