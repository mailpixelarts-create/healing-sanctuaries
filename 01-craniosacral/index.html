Dr. Maya Ravensong Portrait

Fourteen Years Under Dr. John Upledger

Dr. Ravensong spent 14 years studying under Dr. John Upledger, the pioneer of modern craniosacral therapy. Her practice merges osteopathic craniosacral therapy with somatoemotional release, creating a profound healing modality that addresses the deepest layers of stored trauma.

The therapy works with the cerebrospinal fluid rhythm, a subtle tide measurable at 6-12 cycles per minute, to release deep-seated trauma stored in the connective tissue matrix. Each session is a journey into the body's innate wisdom, guided by the quiet pulse of life itself.

Dr. Ravensong's approach honors both the scientific precision of osteopathy and the spiritual dimensions of healing. In her Sedona sanctuary, surrounded by ancient red rocks, patients find a space where the body's self-correcting mechanisms can finally activate.

Healing Modalities

01

Cranial Vault Release

Gentle pressure, equal to the weight of a nickel, applied to cranial bones to restore natural rhythm. This technique releases restrictions in the cranial vault, allowing cerebrospinal fluid to flow freely and the nervous system to find its natural state of balance.

02

Somatoemotional Release

A guided journey through stored trauma held in cellular memory. This technique helps the body re-express and release emotional energies that have become trapped in tissues, often originating from past injuries, surgeries, or life experiences that the body has never fully processed.

03

Spinal Wave Integration

Full-body unwinding following the craniosacral rhythm. The practitioner follows the body's intrinsic movements as the spine begins to undulate and release tension from the base to the crown, restoring the fluid motion that supports neurological and physical well-being.
