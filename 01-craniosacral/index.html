The Process

Before

Arrive

You will be asked to remove your shoes and any heavy jewellery. Lie down on the treatment table fully clothed. Close your eyes. Take three slow breaths. The practitioner will not speak again until the session is complete.

During

Receive

The practitioner places their hands on or just above your body in a series of positions. You may feel warmth, tingling, or pulsing. You may see colours. You may fall asleep. All responses are valid. There is nothing you need to do.

After

Integrate

Sit up slowly. Drink water. The practitioner will share what they sensed during the session. You may feel elated, emotional, or deeply tired. All of this is normal. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours. Sleep if you can.

Questions

What actually happens during a Reiki session?

The practitioner places their hands on or just above your body in a series of positions. They are not applying pressure. They are not manipulating tissue. They are acting as a conduit for universal life force energy — what the Japanese call 'ki.' Your body receives this energy and uses it where it is needed most. Some people feel warmth. Some feel nothing. Both are valid. The effects are cumulative.

How many sessions do I need?

For acute issues: 3–6 sessions. For chronic conditions: 8–12 sessions. For deep spiritual work: ongoing. There is no 'fix.' There is a process. Your body has been holding tension for years — it will not release it in 60 minutes. But you will feel a shift after the first session. Most people do.

Is this the same as massage?

No. Massage manipulates muscle tissue. Reiki works with the energy field that surrounds and penetrates the body. There is no physical manipulation. You remain fully clothed. No oil is used. The practitioner's hands hover or rest gently. The effects are energetic, not mechanical.

Can I combine Reiki with other treatments?

Yes. Reiki complements every form of medicine. It does not interfere with medication, physiotherapy, or counselling. In fact, many hospitals now offer Reiki alongside conventional treatment. The only precaution: avoid alcohol for 24 hours after a session, as your body is still processing.

What if I don't feel anything?

Not feeling anything does not mean it isn't working. Some people are more energetically敏感 than others. Some people fall asleep. Some people see colours. Some feel nothing at all — yet the shift happens at a cellular level. Trust the process. The body knows what to do with the energy, even if your conscious mind doesn't register it.

Why do I feel emotional afterward?

Reiki activates the parasympathetic nervous system — your 'rest and digest' mode. When this system engages, suppressed emotions surface. This is not a side effect — this is the healing. Your body has been storing unprocessed grief, fear, or tension in its tissues. Reiki creates the safety for these emotions to release. Tears are not weakness. They are release.
