The Healer

Dr. Maya Ravensong

Fourteen years studying under Dr. John Upledger shaped a practice that merges osteopathic craniosacral therapy with somatoemotional release. The work listens to the cerebrospinal fluid rhythm — a subtle tide measurable at six to twelve cycles per minute — to release deep-seated trauma stored in the connective tissue matrix.

Dr. Ravensong holds a Doctorate in Osteopathic Medicine from the Michigan State University College of Osteopathic Medicine, with advanced certification from the Upledger Institute International. She completed her craniosacral therapy training in 2011 and has since devoted over 16,000 hours to hands-on clinical practice.

Her approach is rooted in a single conviction: the body already knows how to heal. The therapist's role is not to fix, but to listen — to create the conditions where innate intelligence can do what it has always done. This is not passive work. It is the most attentive thing two people can share.

Each session is a conversation with the body's innate intelligence. No force. No hurry. Only presence.

Sacred Art

Cranial Vault Release

Gentle pressure equal to the weight of a nickel applied to cranial bones. Restoring natural rhythm. Unwinding tension held for years, sometimes decades. The skull is not fixed bone — it breathes, it moves, it remembers. Through micro-movements of the sphenoid and temporal bones, this technique releases restrictions in the cranial vault that affect cerebrospinal fluid flow, bringing relief from chronic headaches, TMJ dysfunction, and the deep fatigue that comes from years of unconscious muscular guarding.

Somatoemotional Release

A guided journey through stored trauma in cellular memory. The body holds what the mind has forgotten. Through careful touch and presence, these held experiences find their way to the surface and release. Using light therapeutic touch combined with dialogue and guided awareness, somatoemotional release works with the body's natural defense systems — the energy cysts and fascial armor — to gently dissolve patterns that have been locked in tissue since the original experience. Clients often report unexpected emotions followed by profound relief.

Spinal Wave Integration

Full-body unwinding following the craniosacral rhythm. A wave moves through the spine, releasing vertebra by vertebra. The body returns to its original pattern — whole, coherent, at ease. This technique engages the dural tube and spinal membranes, allowing the central nervous system to reorganize itself along its natural axis. Each vertebra releases in sequence, like breath moving through water. The result is a resetting of postural patterns and a deep sense of integration — the feeling of being fully in one's body for the first time.

Philosophy

The Approach

01

Listen Before Touch

Every session begins long before hands meet skin. The first act of healing is listening — to breath, to posture, to the subtle rhythms the body broadcasts when it is finally given permission to be heard. Dr. Ravensong spends the opening minutes of each session simply attending to what the body is already saying.

02

No Force, Only Presence

The craniosacral rhythm speaks in whispers. Force cannot hear whispers. This work operates at the threshold of touch — a contact so light it could be mistaken for imagination. It is in this gentleness that the body's deepest defenses finally relax, allowing the kind of release that years of conventional therapy cannot reach.

03

The Body Knows

Beneath the noise of symptoms, diagnoses, and treatment plans, the body holds its own intelligence. A bone knows where it belongs. A membrane knows how to flow. The work of craniosacral therapy is to remove what is in the way and trust what emerges. Healing is not something the therapist does — it is something the body already knows how to do.

Journey

Your First Visit

I

Arrival

Arrive ten minutes early. The treatment room is quiet — dim light, warm air, the scent of cedar. There are no clinical fluorescents here. You will be invited to lie on a padded table, fully clothed. There is nothing to prepare, nothing to perform. Simply arrive.

II

The Session

Dr. Ravensong will begin by resting her hands gently on your head, sacrum, or feet — wherever the rhythm asks to be heard. Sessions last between 60 and 90 minutes. Many clients fall into a state between waking and sleep. Some feel warmth, tingling, or a sense of movement. Others feel nothing at all — and yet something shifts.

III

After

There is no rush to leave. Dr. Ravensong will sit with you for a few minutes as you return. Clients often describe a feeling of spaciousness, as if the room is bigger than it was. Drink water. Rest if you can. The body continues to integrate for hours, sometimes days, after the session ends. This is normal. This is the work continuing.
