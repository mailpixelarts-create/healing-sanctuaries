Seven Generations of Kabiraji Practice

Sensei Tanaka descended from a lineage of Kabiraji practitioners spanning seven generations in Kyoto. The art of Kabiraji involves drawing energy patterns above the body to extract and transmute stagnant chi.

Unlike Reiki, Kabiraji works with visible energy drawing — the practitioner's hands create intricate patterns that are felt as heat, vibration, and release. Each movement is precise, inherited through centuries of practice, and adapted to each individual's unique energy signature.

His Portland sanctuary brings this ancient Japanese healing art to the Pacific Northwest, where the region's natural beauty and contemplative atmosphere provide the perfect setting for deep energetic work and transformation.

The Sacred Art

Healing Modalities

01

Energy Extraction Drawing

Hand movements that draw stagnation from the body's energy field. The practitioner's hands create intricate patterns above the skin, pulling dense, stagnant energy upward and outward, transforming it through specific hand positions passed down through seven generations.

02

Five Point Harmonization

Crystal placement on the five major dantians — the energy centers of the body. Each crystal is selected for its resonance with the specific dantian, creating a harmonic field that supports the body's natural healing processes and restores energetic balance.

03

Ki Flow Restoration

Dynamic movement meditation to restore life force circulation. Inspired by martial arts principles, this practice combines breath work, precise body positioning, and energy channeling to reactivate the body's natural ki flow and restore vitality.
