The Lineage

Sensei Haruki Tanaka

Sensei Tanaka descended from a lineage of Kabiraji practitioners spanning seven generations in Kyoto. The art of Kabiraji involves drawing energy patterns above the body to extract and transmute stagnant chi.

Unlike Reiki, Kabiraji works with visible energy drawing — the practitioner's hands create intricate patterns that are felt as heat, vibration, and release.

After decades of study under his grandmother, Sensei Tanaka brought Kabiraji to the Pacific Northwest, establishing his Portland sanctuary in 2003. His approach blends the precision of classical Japanese energy work with an intuitive understanding of the modern body's unique patterns of holding and stagnation.

Sacred Art

The Way of Kabiraji

Five Principles of
the Kabiraji Path

Kabiraji is more than a healing technique — it is a way of living in right relationship with the energy that moves through all things. These five pillars guide every session and every teaching.

引

Draw, Never Force

The hands invite energy to move. We do not push or manipulate — we create the conditions for stagnation to release on its own. This principle of non-forcing extends to every aspect of healing: the body knows where it needs to go.

間

Honor the Space Between

In Japanese aesthetics, ma — the pause, the emptiness — is where meaning lives. Between each drawing motion, we honor the stillness. The body's greatest transformations happen in the spaces we create.

流

Energy Must Flow

Stagnation is the root of dis-ease. Like water, energy seeks its natural course when given permission. Every Kabiraji session works to restore the body's innate remembering of flow.

根

Return to the Root

Symptoms are whispers. Kabiraji listens for what lies beneath — the original pattern of imbalance that the body has been circling. We trace energy back to its source, addressing cause rather than effect.

和

Harmony Over Cure

The goal is not to "fix" but to restore harmony between all layers of the being — physical, emotional, energetic, and spiritual. When harmony returns, the body's intelligence does the rest.

Your First Visit

What to Expect

Your first session is a gentle introduction to the practice of Kabiraji. There is no pressure, no expectation — only an invitation to experience what your body already knows how to do.

一

Arrival & Settling

Arrive fifteen minutes early. You'll be welcomed into a warm room with the scent of hinoki wood. A cup of ceremonial tea is offered. There is no rush. Sensei Tanaka believes healing begins the moment you decide to slow down.

二

The Conversation

Before any hands-on work, you'll sit with Sensei Tanaka for a quiet conversation about what brought you here. Not a diagnosis — a listening. He will ask about your body, your energy, your life. He listens to what you say and what you don't.

三

The Session

You'll lie fully clothed on a futon. Sensei Tanaka's hands will move above and around your body, drawing energy patterns you can feel as warmth, tingling, or deep release. Some people feel movement; others feel stillness. Both are signs of the practice working.

四

Integration

After the session, you'll rest for a few minutes. A second cup of tea is offered. Sensei Tanaka will share what he noticed — patterns, flows, areas of stagnation — and offer guidance for your continued healing between sessions.

A note on pricing: Your first visit is offered at a reduced rate of $95. Subsequent sessions are $140 for ninety minutes. A sliding scale is available for those experiencing financial hardship — healing should never be gatekept by income.
