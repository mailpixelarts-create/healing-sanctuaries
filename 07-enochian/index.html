The Mechanism

Scent is the only sense with direct access to the limbic system — the brain's centre for emotion and memory. What you breathe, your emotions feel. What you smell, your soul understands.

Odorant molecules bind to olfactory receptors in the nasal epithelium, which project directly to the amygdala (emotion processing) and hippocampus (memory consolidation). Lavender's primary compound, linalool, increases GABA neurotransmitter activity by up to 40%, producing measurable anxiolytic effects within seconds of inhalation.

This is not placebo. This is neurochemistry. The nose-brain pathway is the fastest of all sensory channels — scent reaches the limbic system in as little as three seconds, bypassing the thalamic relay that all other senses must travel through.

Questions

What actually happens during an aromatherapy session?

Flora first conducts a detailed olfactory consultation — asking about your relationship with scent, your emotional landscape, and your physical sensations. She then selects from over two hundred botanical essences to create a bespoke blend. The blend is applied to pulse points — wrists, temples, heart — or diffused in the treatment room. You may feel warmth, emotional release, or deep relaxation. The effects begin within three seconds of inhalation.

How many sessions do I need?

For acute stress or emotional processing: 3–6 sessions. For chronic insomnia, anxiety, or pain management: 8–12 sessions. For deep somatic work and personal mythology exploration: ongoing. Each session builds upon the last, as your olfactory library unfolds layer by layer. Most clients report a significant shift after the first session.

Is aromatherapy the same as using essential oils at home?

No. Using essential oils at home is like reading sheet music versus hearing an orchestra. Flora's blends are precisely calibrated therapeutic formulations — specific chemotypes, specific dilutions, specific synergies. A bottle of lavender from the shop contains one compound. Flora's lavender blend may contain seven species, each chosen for a distinct neurological effect. The difference is clinical.

Can I combine aromatherapy with other treatments?

Yes. Aromatherapy complements every modality — massage, counselling, physiotherapy, medication. It does not interfere with pharmaceutical treatments. Many clients use their bespoke blends alongside conventional therapy. The only precaution: avoid applying undiluted oils to broken skin, and certain oils (eucalyptus, rosemary) are contraindicated for epilepsy. Flora will advise on all safety considerations during your consultation.

What if I don't feel anything?

Not feeling anything does not mean it isn't working. The olfactory-limbic pathway operates below conscious awareness. Your body is processing the chemical signals even if your mind does not register a specific sensation. Some people experience vivid memories, emotional release, or physical warmth. Others feel nothing at all — yet their cortisol levels drop, their heart rate slows, and their sleep improves. Trust the process. The nose knows, even when the mind does not.

Why do essential oils need to be diluted?

Undiluted essential oils are highly concentrated chemical compounds that can cause skin irritation, sensitisation, or chemical burns. Proper dilution in a carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond) ensures safe absorption, optimises therapeutic efficacy, and allows the volatile compounds to release gradually over hours rather than seconds. Flora uses clinical-grade dilution ratios — typically 2–3% for adults — based on your specific needs and skin sensitivity.
