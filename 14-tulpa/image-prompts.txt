### Services Section — Three Cards
Three vertical panels showing art therapy practices:
1. **Scribble**: A hand holding a piece of black charcoal, making rapid, messy scribbles on white paper. The scribbles are aggressive, raw, unfiltered. The charcoal leaves thick, dark marks. The motion is visible in the blur of the hand.
2. **Clay Work**: Two hands pressing into a lump of wet clay on a table. The clay is being shaped — not into anything recognizable, but into a form that represents weight, pressure, emotion. The hands are strong, deliberate.
3. **Blind Contour**: A person drawing their own hand without looking at the paper. The eyes are fixed on the hand; the charcoal moves on the paper below. The resulting drawing is wobbly, imperfect, honest. The person smiles at the result.
Maximalist palette — white paper, bold colors, raw materials. 3:1 triptych.

### Process Section — Three Steps
Three images showing the Scribble to Self session:
1. **The Blank Page**: A large sheet of white paper on a table. A hand reaches for a piece of charcoal. The paper is empty, waiting. The moment before the first mark — the moment of pure potential. Close-up, bright light.
2. **The Scribble**: The same paper, now covered in rapid, aggressive scribbles. The charcoal has been pressed hard — the marks are deep, dark, visceral. The hand is still moving, fast, uncontrolled. The scribble is not art; it is release.
3. **The Question**: The finished scribble lies on the table. It is a mess of lines, angles, and pressure. A hand rests beside it, still holding the charcoal. The question hangs in the air: "What did your hand know that your mouth didn't?" The answer is in the marks. The marks are the truth.
Maximalist outsider palette — black charcoal on white paper, raw, honest. 1:1 per image.
