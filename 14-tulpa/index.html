The Mechanism

Art bypasses the Wernicke's area (language center). It directly accesses the anterior cingulate cortex (emotional regulation) via somatic markers—emotions embedded in proprioceptive memory.

When you paint, draw, or sculpt, your brain shifts from beta to alpha and theta states. The prefrontal cortex quiets. The amygdala stops filtering. What emerges is not art—it is truth, externalized.

The Process

Before

No planning. No Pinterest boards. No expectations. Arrive with whatever you're carrying—joy, rage, grief, numbness. All emotions are welcome at the canvas.

During

20-minute spontaneous mark-making. Eyes closed for the first five minutes. Let the body choose the colors. Let the hand move without the mind's permission. The canvas receives everything.

After

Title the piece. Do not judge it. Sit with what was created. Name what arose. We carry the colors home as medicine. The painting becomes a mirror you didn't know you needed.

Questions

"I can't draw. Will this work for me?"

Perfect. You will make marks, not pictures. Art therapy is not about skill—it's about expression. A two-year-old can paint. So can you. The canvas doesn't care about talent. It cares about truth.

"Will I cry?"

Possibly. That is the release. Tears are not weakness—they are the body's way of letting go of what words couldn't carry. Many people cry. Some laugh. Some sit in silence. All responses are valid.

"Can I do this with my kids?"

Absolutely. Children naturally bypass language. They paint before they speak. Art therapy meets them where they already live—in the land of color and feeling. Family sessions available.

"Is this the same as an art class?"

No. Art classes teach technique. Art therapy teaches release. There is no teacher here—only a witness. You won't learn to paint a landscape. You'll learn to externalize your inner world in color and form.

"What if I don't feel anything?"

Numbness is a valid response. Sometimes the body needs time to trust the process. The act of making marks—even when it feels empty—is still rewiring neural pathways. Come back. The feeling will arrive.

"How is this different from therapy?"

Traditional talk therapy works through language. Art therapy works through the body. When words fail, colors speak. When the mind blocks, the hand knows. We complement clinical therapy—we don't replace it.
