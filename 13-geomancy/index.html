02 — The Mechanism

How Qi Gong
Actually Works

Qi Gong manipulates the interstitial fluid — the 20% of water not in blood cells. Specific slow movements create mechanical shear stress, which increases ATP (energy) production in the mitochondria.

This is not metaphor. This is cellular biology. The "energy" you feel is real — it is the hum of your cells waking up. The micro-circulation improvements measurable within a single session.

After practice, hold your hands apart. Feel the warmth. That is Qi — interstitial fluid responding to intentional movement. The body knows how to heal. We simply remove the obstacles.

08 — Frequently Asked

Questions &
Answers

Why is Qi Gong performed so slowly?
Speed is not power; reach is power. Slow movement allows the body to actually feel what is happening. You cannot sense qi at the speed of stress. The slowness is precision — it gives your nervous system time to register and respond to each micro-shift in posture and breath.
Can I practice Qi Gong with an existing injury?
Yes, adapt movement to your pain-free range. Qi Gong meets you where you are. The practice heals — it does not harm. Master Liang regularly modifies sequences for students with knee replacements, spinal fusions, and chronic conditions. Always consult your physician first.
What is the warming sensation in my hands?
That is Qi — the warming of interstitial fluid. The increased circulation creates a palpable field of energy between your palms. It is measurable: thermal imaging shows temperature increases of 2–4°C in the hands after a single session. This is biology, not mysticism.
How is Qi Gong different from Tai Chi?
Tai Chi is a martial art with complex choreographed forms. Qi Gong is simpler, more meditative, and focused specifically on health cultivation. Think of Tai Chi as a novel and Qi Gong as poetry — both are beautiful, but Qi Gong is more accessible for beginners and healing-focused practitioners.
How quickly will I notice results?
Most students feel a shift in their first session — lighter shoulders, deeper breath, a sense of calm. Structural changes in circulation and ATP production begin within weeks of consistent practice. Master Liang recommends three sessions per week for twelve weeks to establish a foundation.
Is Qi Gong a religion or spiritual practice?
Qi Gong is neither a religion nor a philosophy — it is a technology of the body. While its roots touch Daoist and Buddhist traditions, the practice itself is purely physical and energetic. People of all faiths and none practice Qi Gong. The only belief required is that your body can heal itself.
