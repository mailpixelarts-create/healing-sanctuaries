The Method

The 3-Step Process

Before

Study the Origin

Study the plant's geographic origin. Know the soil, the altitude, the season of harvest. Context determines chemistry.

During

Brew at 90°C

Brew at 90°C — never boiling. Excessive heat destroys volatile compounds and degrades the very molecules that heal.

After

Observe for 4 Hours

Observe the body's subtle reaction over 4 hours. The body speaks in whispers — learn its language.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked

"Is this just tea?"

No. It's a targeted molecular intervention. Each formulation delivers specific phyto-compounds to specific receptor sites. The dose, the timing, and the combination matter as much as the plant itself.

"Can I mix herbs?"

Only if you know the synergies. Some herbs amplify each other; others cancel or interfere. A trained herbalist understands these molecular relationships — what pharmacologists call "drug-herb interactions" is simply the plant speaking its own language.

"Can I use them with medication?"

Always inform your physician. Herbal compounds can alter pharmaceutical metabolism through cytochrome P450 pathways. Safety requires transparency between practitioner and doctor.

"How long until I feel results?"

Acute conditions may respond within days. Chronic patterns often require 4 to 8 weeks of consistent use. The body heals in its own time — our role is to support the process, not force it.

"Are herbs safe for children?"

Many herbs are gentle enough for children at appropriate doses. However, children are not small adults — their metabolisms differ. Always work with a qualified practitioner for pediatric formulations.

"Where do you source your plants?"

Wildcrafted from the Pacific Northwest when possible, from land Rowan knows intimately. Supplemental herbs come from organic farms she has visited personally. Every specimen is identified, harvested, and processed by hand.
