[09] // FREQUENTLY ASKED
Q: "Does acupuncture hurt?"
The insertion is a micro-pinch — the needles are hair-thin (0.16mm). The "dull ache" you may feel is called Deqi, the arrival of Qi, which signals endorphin release. Most patients describe it as a deep, satisfying pressure rather than pain.
Q: "How many sessions do I need?"
Acute conditions often resolve in 4–6 sessions. Chronic conditions typically require 10–12 sessions. Your body sets the pace — we follow its lead. Many patients continue monthly maintenance sessions for ongoing wellness.
Q: "Why does treating my hand heal my organs?"
Meridians are conductive pathways — energy travels along mapped routes, not just local tissue. The Large Intestine 4 point (LI4) on your hand connects to your face, head, and immune system. These pathways have been mapped for 3,000 years and confirmed by modern fMRI studies.
Q: "Is acupuncture safe?"
When performed by a licensed practitioner, acupuncture is exceptionally safe. We use sterile, single-use needles. Side effects are rare and mild — occasional bruising or brief lightheadedness. It is covered by most insurance plans and endorsed by the WHO.
Q: "Can acupuncture help with fertility?"
Acupuncture has been shown to improve IVF success rates by 40–60% in clinical studies. It regulates reproductive hormones, improves uterine blood flow, and reduces stress — all critical factors in conception. We work alongside your reproductive endocrinologist.
Q: "What should I do before my first session?"
Eat a light meal 1–2 hours before. Avoid caffeine and alcohol. Wear loose, comfortable clothing. Arrive 10 minutes early to settle in. Come with an open mind — your body already knows how to heal. The needles simply remind it.
