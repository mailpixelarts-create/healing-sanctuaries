La Tradición

The Ancient Medicine of Our Ancestors

Maestra Rosa carries the medicine wheel of her ancestors, blending Aztec, Mayan, and Spanish Catholic healing traditions. Curanderismo treats the whole person — body, mind, and soul — through limpias (spiritual cleansings), herbal remedies, and ceremony. It is a living tradition, passed down through generations, rooted in the belief that healing begins when the soul remembers its wholeness.

Las Prácticas Sagradas

Sacred Healing Arts

01

Limpia

Egg Limpia

Sacred egg rolled over the body to absorb negative energy. The egg is passed in careful motions, drawing out stagnation and illness. Afterward, cracked into water to read the signs — a practice as old as the mountains themselves.

02

Baño

Herbal Bath Ceremony

Botanical preparations for spiritual purification. Fresh herbs — rosemary, rue, basil, marigold — are gathered at dawn, simmered in warm water, and poured over the body while prayers are spoken. Each plant carries its own spirit, its own medicine.

03

Alma

Soul Retrieval Ceremony

A guided journey to recover fragmented soul parts lost to trauma, grief, or shock. Through drumming, prayer, and sacred smoke, the healer travels between worlds to call back what was lost — restoring the light that rightfully belongs to each person.

The egg knows what the body forgets. The smoke carries what the word cannot hold. Healing is not fixing — it is remembering.

— Maestra Rosa Esperanza
