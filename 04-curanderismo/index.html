About Thich Minh Hanh

Thich Minh Hanh studied under the revered Thich Nhat Hanh at Plum Village, the global center of mindfulness practice in the south of France. For over twenty years, he has devoted his life to teaching the art of conscious living.

His approach integrates ancient Zen traditions with modern mindfulness techniques, creating a practice that is both profound and accessible to contemporary seekers.

Thich teaches that meditation is not an escape from life but a deep engagement with it — a way to touch the miracle of being alive in each breath.

Sacred Art of Meditation

01

Mindful Breathing

The foundation of all practice. Following the breath as it moves through the body, anchoring awareness in the present. Each inhale is a homecoming, each exhale a letting go.

◎
02

Body Scan

A systematic journey through the physical form, releasing tension held in muscle and bone. The body becomes a landscape of sensation, each area a territory of awareness.

◉
03

Walking Meditation

Movement as meditation. Each step becomes a deliberate act of presence, transforming ordinary motion into a sacred ritual. The path is the practice.

Philosophy

Present Moment Awareness

The past is a memory, the future is a projection. Only the now is real. We train the mind to rest here, in the immediacy of experience.

Non-Attachment

Observing thoughts and emotions without clinging or resistance. They arise, they pass. We are the sky, not the clouds.

Compassionate Inquiry

Looking deeply into the nature of suffering with kindness. Understanding transforms. The wound becomes the door to wisdom.

Embodied Stillness

Stillness is not the absence of movement but its deepest expression. In the body, we find the ground of being.

Interbeing

We are not separate. The breath connects us to all beings. Meditation reveals the profound interdependence of all life.

Your Journey

1

Arrival

Begin at the door. Leave the outside world behind. Take a moment to simply stand, to breathe, to arrive fully in this space.

2

Settling

Fifteen minutes of silent sitting. No instruction, no guidance. Just you and the breath, finding your natural rhythm together.

3

Guided Practice

Thich leads the group through one of the three sacred arts. Voice soft, pace slow, presence deep.

4

Sharing Circle

An invitation to speak, to listen, to witness. No advice, no fixing. Just the profound gift of being heard.

5

Closing Bell

Three bells. The first for the past, released. The second for the future, unborn. The third for this moment, alive.
